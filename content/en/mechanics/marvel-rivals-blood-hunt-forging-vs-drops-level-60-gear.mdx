## marvel rivals blood hunt forging vs drops level 60 gear: Which Method Wins?

For level 60 optimization, forging is the stronger default route. Open-world/combat drops still matter, but mostly as bonus lottery wins and shard fuel.

### Drop route strengths and limits

Enemy drops start becoming meaningful around Nightmare 20–25, then improve as difficulty increases. You can absolutely get excellent legendaries from combat, including very high perk quality. The catch: many drops arrive below your current character level.

So if your character is 60, a lot of loot may still land at 50, with fewer level 60 pieces mixed in. That is why drops feel exciting but unreliable for targeted upgrades.

Forging lets you choose the character, slot, and best available craft level. At endgame, this is what gives you controlled progression. Instead of waiting for the perfect chest or gauntlet, you can repeatedly roll that slot.

## Step-by-Step Forging Workflow for Consistent Level 60 Upgrades

Use this loop every session to avoid wasted shards and cluttered inventory:

1. **Filter and scrap low-value inventory**  
   Start by removing normal/green/blue pieces and any purple/orange items that do not fit your current build plan.

2. **Lock your target slot first**  
   Pick one weak slot (example: chest with low survivability stats) and only craft for that slot until improved.

3. **Craft at “Best” item level**  
   Always select the highest available crafting level. At endgame, this should align with your current character cap.

4. **Batch craft instead of single pulls**  
   Save enough Uru shards for multi-craft sessions (commonly 10). This gives clearer value tracking and faster iteration.

5. **Compare by impact, not rarity hype**  
   Check active perk synergy, core stat gains, and utility value (healing cooldown, armor, health, etc.).

6. **Recycle misses immediately**  
   Failed rolls become more Uru fuel. The loop sustains itself if you stay disciplined.

| Crafting Step | Goal | Common mistake | Better move |
|---|---|---|---|
| Inventory cleanup | Generate shard pool | Keeping “maybe” gear forever | Scrap aggressively |
| Slot targeting | Solve real weakness | Random crafting across slots | Commit to one slot |
| Level selection | Max scaling value | Crafting below best level | Always use **Best** |
| Batch size | Smooth RNG variance | Crafting one-by-one | Save for 10+ attempts |

> **💡 Tip:** If one slot is still weak after several sessions, stop chasing “perfect” and equip the best functional upgrade now. Survivability spikes often come from good-enough improvements.
