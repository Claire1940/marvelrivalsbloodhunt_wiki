## Choosing Your Hero and Understanding Progression

The first step in your Blood Hunt journey is selecting a hero. As of 2026, the roster for this PVE event features six distinct characters, including newcomers like Moon Knight and Squirrel Girl. It is vital to understand that progression in this mode is **character-specific**. If you spend hours leveling up Moon Knight and reaching high Nightmare tiers, those upgrades do not carry over to the Punisher or Captain America. You will need to level up each hero individually to unlock their full potential.

| Hero | Role/Playstyle | Key Strength |
|---|---|---|
| **Moon Knight** | Melee Brawler | High sustain through life leech and close-quarters combat. |
| **Squirrel Girl** | Crowd Control | Excellent at managing large groups of smaller vampires. |
| **The Punisher** | Ranged DPS | High single-target damage, ideal for burning down bosses. |
| **Captain America** | Tank/Support | Provides damage mitigation and can withstand heavy hits. |

Your initial goal should be to perform four to five clears on **Normal mode**. This allows you to gain basic experience points and start filling out your trait nodes. Once you feel comfortable, move to Hard, and eventually Extreme. Extreme is considered the first major checkpoint because it introduces you to the full rotation of bosses and mechanics you will face in the endgame.

## The Difficulty Ladder and Boss Encounters

Blood Hunt features a tiered difficulty system that rewards players with progressively better gear. Each difficulty level adds new sections to the map and introduces tougher bosses with unique telegraphs.

1.  **Normal Mode**: A quick 5-minute run culminating in a fight against **Capwolf** (Captain America: Werewolf Edition).
2.  **Hard Mode**: Extends the run through the subway systems to face **Ratatoskr**, the vampiric squirrel god.
3.  **Extreme Mode**: The full experience, leading you to Dracula’s Castle for the final showdown with the Lord of Vampires.
4.  **Nightmare Tiers**: Unlocked after Extreme, these are wave-based challenges that test your endgame builds.

> **💡 Tip:** Always watch for red pathways on the ground during boss fights. These telegraphs indicate high-damage attacks that can often one-shot under-leveled heroes.

### Unlocking the Secret Boss
While running Extreme mode, keep a close eye on the top of your screen for **Optional Objectives**. These may include destroying "Blood Root Flowers" or clearing specific enemy camps before moving to the next area. Completing all optional objectives before reaching Dracula will unlock a hidden boss encounter that offers exclusive high-tier loot.

## Building Your Character: Skill Trees and Arcana

The skill tree system in Blood Hunt is divided into columns that significantly alter your abilities. A common mistake for beginners is trying to be a "Jack of All Trades." In this mode, efficiency comes from **compounding percentages**. It is highly recommended to pick one specific column and "full send" your points down that path.

### Arcana Cards and Realm Level
There are two types of Arcana to manage. The first is your **Arcana Realm Level**, which provides permanent buffs like the Scrolls of Conquest or Scrolls of Immortality. These stay with you across all runs. 

The second type is the **in-run Arcana cards**. At the start of every match and between every wave, you can choose from three random cards. These provide temporary buffs for that specific run.

| Card Type | Effect | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Close Quarters** | Increases damage to enemies within 5 meters. | Melee builds (Moon Knight). |
| **Indomitable** | Provides a flat 5% damage reduction. | Tank builds or high-difficulty survival. |
| **Ultimate Charge** | Increases the rate at which your Ultimate builds. | Ability-spam builds. |

## Gear Management and the Forge System

Gear is the backbone of your power in the later stages of the Blood Hunt. Gear drops are specific to the character you are currently playing, though you will occasionally find pieces for other heroes as you reach higher levels.

### Understanding Gear Rarity
Not all gear is created equal. The rarity of an item determines how many **Extended Effects** (perks) it can hold.

| Rarity | Number of Perks | Stat Potential |
|---|---|---|
| **White** | 0 | Low base stats only. |
| **Green** | 1 | Single C-tier or D-tier perk. |
| **Blue** | 2 | Multiple B-tier or C-tier perks. |
| **Purple** | 3 | High-tier A/B perks. |
| **Legendary (Orange)** | 3+ | Unique effects and S-tier stat rolls. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not simply equip gear with the highest rating. A lower-level item with a 250% bonus to close-range damage is often better for a melee build than a higher-level item with useless perks.

### Scrapping and Forging
As you accumulate hundreds of pieces of junk gear, use the **Forge tab** to clean your inventory.
*   **Scrap**: Smelt unwanted items into **Uru Shards**. Use the filter tool to quickly select all White and Green items for mass destruction.
*   **Forge**: Use your Uru Shards to craft specific gear slots. This is the best way to "plug a gap" in your build if you are missing a high-level accessory or weapon.

## Combat Survival: Healing and Revives

Survival in the Blood Hunt requires careful management of your resources. Every hero has three charges of a healing ability that restores a chunk of health and provides health-over-time. These charges regenerate, so you should use them proactively rather than waiting for your health to hit critical levels.

### Lifeline Serums
Players start with a stack of **Lifeline Serums**, which allow for instant revives. However, these are a finite resource that costs blue units to replenish. 
*   **When to use**: Only use a serum if you are on the final boss, the boss is at low health, and the timer is running out.
*   **When to skip**: If the boss still has 75% HP and is currently "Enraged" (doubled damage), save your serum. It is better to fail the run and keep your gear than to waste expensive revives on a lost cause.

### Hunting Loot Goblins
During waves, keep an eye out for small, yellow glowing creatures crawling on the floor. These are **Loot Goblins**. They do not attack, but they will flee the arena quickly. Prioritize killing them immediately, as they are guaranteed to drop a massive amount of gear, often including Purple or Legendary items.

## FAQ: Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt Beginner Guide

**Q: Do I lose my gear if I fail a run in the Blood Hunt?**

A: No. Any gear you pick up during a run is permanently added to your inventory, even if your team wipes. This makes it worth attempting higher difficulties even if you aren't sure you can finish them.

**Q: How do I beat the Dracula boss fight?**

A: Dracula has two main phases to watch for. When he disappears and spawns vampires, your goal is to destroy the **five floating swords** in the arena. In his final phase, you must break the **two chained crystals** near his throne to remove his invulnerability shield.

**Q: What is the fastest way to level up my Arcana Realm?**

A: The fastest way to gain experience is to play on the highest difficulty you can comfortably clear. Extreme mode offers the best balance of time investment and experience gain for mid-level players.

**Q: Are there any "must-have" perks for every build?**

A: While builds vary, **Life Leech** is universally powerful in this **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt beginner guide** context. Being able to restore health simply by dealing damage significantly reduces your reliance on healing charges and serums.
