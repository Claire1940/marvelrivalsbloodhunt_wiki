## Getting Started with Blood Hunt Progression

Unlike the standard PvP modes, Blood Hunt features a dedicated progression system that is unique to each hero. If you reach level 50 with Moon Knight, your Squirrel Girl will still start at level 1. This means you must carefully choose which character to invest in first. Your journey begins in Normal mode, where you will face Capwolf, a werewolf version of Captain America.

As you progress through the difficulties, the length of the runs and the complexity of the bosses increase. While Normal mode is a quick five-minute sprint, Extreme mode can take up to 25 minutes as you navigate through subway systems and eventually reach Dracula’s Castle.

| Difficulty | Approximate Time | Key Boss | Unlock Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Normal** | 5 Minutes | Capwolf | None (Starting Mode) |
| **Hard** | 12 Minutes | Ratatosker | Clear Normal 4-5 times |
| **Extreme** | 25 Minutes | Dracula | Clear Hard Mode |
| **Nightmare** | Varies | Multiple Bosses | Clear Extreme Mode |

> **💡 Tip:** Do not rush into Hard or Extreme mode immediately. Spend time in Normal to gather a baseline set of Level 1-8 gear before attempting higher tiers.

## Understanding Gear Rarities and Stats

Loot drops from enemies constantly during waves. While it is tempting to simply equip the item with the highest power rating, the "Extended Effects" are what actually determine a build's viability. Gear is categorized by color, and each rarity level adds more potential for bonus stats.

| Gear Color | Rarity | Extended Effects | Potential Stat Tiers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **White** | Normal | 0 | None |
| **Green** | Uncommon | 1 | D - C |
| **Blue** | Rare | 2 | C - B |
| **Purple** | Epic | 3 | B - A |
| **Orange** | Legendary | 4+ | A - S |

When inspecting gear, look for the tier of the effect. For example, a "Bonus Damage against Close-Range Enemies" perk might offer 129% at D-tier, but a B-tier version of the same perk could offer a staggering 250%. Always prioritize the stat synergy over the base item level.

## Mastering the Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt Forge

The **marvel rivals blood hunt forge** is divided into two primary functions: Scrap and Forge. Because your inventory will quickly fill with hundreds of items, you must learn to filter and smelt efficiently. Accessing the **marvel rivals blood hunt forge** menu between runs is the most effective way to keep your power level climbing.

### The Scrap Process (Smelting)
Smelting converts unwanted gear into **Uru Shards**, the primary currency for crafting. To save time, use the filter tool to select all "Normal" and "Uncommon" items. You can then "Select All" and smelt them instantly. Be careful to deselect items for other characters you might want to play later, as gear is character-specific but the Uru Shards are shared across your account.

### The Forge Process (Crafting)
If you have a high-level chest piece but are still using a level 10 accessory, you can use the **marvel rivals blood hunt forge** to target that specific slot. 
1. Select the **Forge** tab.
2. Choose the specific gear slot (e.g., Accessory, Weapon, Armor).
3. Select the Hero you are crafting for.
4. Set the item level (this defaults to your current highest possible level).
5. Spend Uru Shards to "roll" for a new item.

The results are randomized. You might craft a White item that goes straight back into the scrap heap, or you might land a Purple Epic with three B-tier stats that perfectly complement your melee or ranged build. Utilizing the **marvel rivals blood hunt forge** is the only reliable way to "plug the gaps" in your equipment set without relying purely on RNG drops from enemies.

## Boss Mechanics and Hidden Objectives

Every boss in Blood Hunt has telegraphed attacks marked by red pathways on the ground. Avoiding these is critical, especially in Nightmare mode where a single hit can be fatal. However, some mechanics are less obvious.

*   **Capwolf:** Focus on mobility. He lunges frequently, so stay behind him.
*   **Ratatosker:** The vampiric squirrel god uses area-of-effect (AoE) corruption. Watch the floor patterns closely.
*   **Dracula:** This fight has multiple phases. When he disappears and spawns minions, look for the **five floating swords**. You must destroy these to bring him back to the field. In his final stand, he will drop a massive bubble; break the two **chained crystals** near his throne to remove his invulnerability.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be stingy with your Lifeline Serums. These free revives are limited. Only use them if the boss is below 25% health and you are certain you can finish the kill.

## Arcana and Skill Tree Synergy

Your character's power isn't just in their gear. The Arcana system provides both permanent and temporary buffs. 
*   **Permanent Arcana:** These are leveled up via your Realm Level and provide flat stat increases like Scrolls of Conquest or Immortality.
*   **In-Run Arcana (Cards):** At the start of every match and between waves, you can press the interact key (G on PC) to choose from three random cards. 

For a melee-focused Moon Knight build, always look for "Close Quarters" cards or "Damage Mitigation." If you are running a life-leech build, prioritize cards that increase your healing received or attack speed. You can find more details on the [official Marvel Rivals website](https://www.marvelrivals.com/) regarding seasonal balance changes to these perks.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more Uru Shards for the marvel rivals blood hunt forge?**

A: The best way to get Uru Shards is to smelt unwanted gear in the Scrap tab. You should also hunt for "Loot Goblins"—yellow glowing enemies that crawl on the floor during waves. They drop a massive amount of gear that can be smelted down.

**Q: Can I use gear I found on Moon Knight for my Squirrel Girl?**

A: No. Gear is specific to the hero you were playing when it dropped. However, you can use the forge to craft level 1 gear for a new hero using the Uru Shards you earned on your main character, giving your alts a significant head start.

**Q: What is the secret boss in Extreme mode?**

A: To unlock the secret boss, you must complete optional objectives that appear at the top of your screen during the Extreme run before reaching Dracula. These usually involve destroying "Blood Root Flowers" or clearing specific elite mobs.

**Q: Is "Restorative Respire" a good perk to use?**

A: Generally, no. While it provides health regeneration out of combat, it is often too slow for the fast-paced nature of Blood Hunt. You are better off focusing on "Life Leech" or using your three active healing charges (X on PC) more effectively.
