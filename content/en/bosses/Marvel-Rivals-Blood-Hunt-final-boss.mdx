## Who is the Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt Final Boss?

After the intense battle with Dracula and the recovery of the North Stone, players might expect a moment of respite. Instead, they are met by Wilson Fisk, the Mayor of New York, who frames the heroes' vigilante actions as a violation of the law. Fisk serves as the ultimate encounter in this mode, utilizing a combination of high-tech weaponry, political influence, and raw physical power.

Unlike previous encounters that focused on supernatural threats, the **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt final boss** fight is a battle of attrition and positioning. Fisk utilizes a massive mech-suit or enhanced armor that allows him to execute "Secret Strikes" and unleash "Unlimited Power" surges that can wipe an unprepared team in seconds.

## Phase 1: The Mayor's Arrest Warrant

In the initial stage of the fight, Fisk remains relatively stationary, using his authority and long-range firepower to keep the team at bay. He will frequently call out "Secret Strike," which targets a specific player with a high-damage orbital or mechanical blast.

| Ability Name | Effect | How to Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Secret Strike** | High-damage targeted projectile. | Use **dashes** or shields to mitigate impact. |
| **Justice Enforcement** | Fisk fires a volley of rockets in a cone. | Stay behind cover or move to his **flanks**. |
| **The North Stone Pulse** | A knockback wave that pushes heroes away. | Maintain distance or use **stability** buffs. |

> **Warning:** Do not stand directly in front of Fisk during the "Secret Strike" animation. The telegraph is short, and the damage can one-shot squishier DPS characters on Extreme difficulty.

## Phase 2: Bending Your Will

Once Fisk's health drops below 60%, his tactics shift. He begins to leverage the North Stone's energy more aggressively, attempting to "break the bones and bend the will" of the heroes. This phase introduces a crowd-control mechanic that can turn teammates against each other if not managed correctly.

During this phase, the **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt final boss** will frequently shout, "If I can't break your bones, I'll bend your will instead." This indicates a mind-control or heavy suppression aura.

| Mechanic | Description | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Will Bending** | A tether connects Fisk to a random hero. | Break the tether by moving **out of line-of-sight**. |
| **Unlimited Power** | Fisk enters an energized state with increased DR. | Focus fire on the **Stone Capacitors** around the arena. |
| **Flamethrower Burst** | A short-range fire attack that leaves a DoT. | Healers must prioritize **cleansing** the burn effect. |

## Best Team Composition for Blood Hunt

Beating the **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt final boss** requires a balanced team that can handle both high burst damage and sustained healing. On Extreme difficulty, a "glass cannon" approach will likely result in a quick defeat.

1.  **The Tank (Vanguard):** Doctor Strange or Magneto. Their ability to provide team-wide shields is essential for surviving the "Unlimited Power" phases.
2.  **The Healer (Strategist):** Luna Snow or Mantis. You need consistent, high-output healing to counter the "Fire" and "Will Bend" damage-over-time effects.
3.  **The DPS (Duelist):** Iron Man or Namor. High mobility is key to avoiding Fisk's ground-targeted strikes.
4.  **The Flex:** Black Panther or Spider-Man. Having a hero who can quickly move to deactivate Stone Capacitors is vital for shortening the second phase.

## Strategy for Extreme Difficulty

On Extreme Difficulty, the **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt final boss** gains significantly more health and his abilities have much shorter cooldowns. The "Secret Strike" becomes almost constant, requiring the team to stay in a permanent state of motion.

*   **Environmental Awareness:** The arena features several pillars. Use these to break the line of sight when Fisk begins his "Will Bending" chant. 
*   **Capacitor Priority:** When Fisk shouts "Unlimited Power," he becomes nearly invulnerable. Your team must immediately stop attacking Fisk and destroy the three glowing capacitors that emerge from the floor.
*   **Ultimate Management:** Save your Ultimates for the final 20% of his health. Fisk becomes much more aggressive in his "Desperation Phase," and you need to burn through his remaining HP before his "Justice" mechanics overwhelm your healers.

> **Tip:** Coordination is key. Designate one player to call out when the "Secret Strike" is targeting the backline so healers can pre-emptively shield.

## Final Phase: The Mayor's Last Stand

When Fisk reaches 10% health, he will stop using his mechanical gadgets and rely on raw North Stone energy. He will repeatedly scream "I WILL CRUSH YOU" and "YOU CAN'T ESCAPE JUSTICE." During this time, the entire arena will be under a constant "Fire" effect. 

This is a pure DPS check. Your team must stay grouped within a healing aura while focusing all fire on Fisk. Ignore any remaining adds and focus entirely on the **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt final boss**. Once his HP hits zero, a cinematic will trigger where Fisk laments that New Yorkers will always remember him as the one who gave them everything.

## FAQ

**Q: Can the Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt final boss be soloed?**

A: On Normal difficulty, it is possible with high-sustain heroes like Venom, but on Extreme difficulty, the mechanics require at least three players to manage the capacitors and tethers simultaneously.

**Q: What happens if I fail the "Will Bend" mechanic?**

A: If the tether is not broken within 5 seconds, the targeted player will be stunned for 8 seconds and take massive psychic damage, often leading to an immediate knockout.

**Q: Is Wilson Fisk the only boss in the Blood Hunt event?**

A: No, players must first defeat Dracula in the previous stage before facing the **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt final boss**, Wilson Fisk.

**Q: Does the North Stone have any gameplay effects for the player?**

A: During the final cinematic, the North Stone is recovered, but it does not grant players active abilities during the actual boss fight; it primarily serves as a narrative device and a power source for Fisk's mechanics.
