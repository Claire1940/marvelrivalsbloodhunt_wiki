## Primary Fixes for Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt Crashing

If your hardware meets the specifications but you are still facing **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt crashing**, the issue likely lies within your software configuration or game installation. Follow these steps in order to isolate and resolve the problem.

### 1. Verify Game File Integrity
Corrupted files are a common culprit for crashes that occur during map loading or character selection. If a specific asset for the Blood Hunt update didn't download correctly, the game will force-close when it tries to access it.

*   **Steam Users:** Right-click Marvel Rivals > Properties > Installed Files > **Verify integrity of game files**.
*   **Epic Games Users:** Click the three dots (...) next to the game title > Manage > **Verify**.

### 2. Update Graphics Drivers
NVIDIA and AMD frequently release "Game Ready" drivers specifically optimized for Marvel Rivals updates. Using drivers from 2025 or earlier will almost certainly result in **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt crashing** due to shader incompatibilities.

### 3. Clear DirectX Shader Cache
Sometimes, the compiled shaders become corrupted after a major patch like Blood Hunt. Clearing the cache forces the game to rebuild them, often resolving "D3D Device Lost" errors.

1. Type **Disk Cleanup** in your Windows search bar.
2. Select your C: drive.
3. Check the box for **DirectX Shader Cache** and click OK.
4. Restart your PC and relaunch the game.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Impact on Stability |

## Advanced Troubleshooting Steps

If the standard fixes do not stop the **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt crashing**, you may need to look at deeper system conflicts. Marvel Rivals is built on a modern engine that requires specific background environments to function correctly.

### Disable Overlays and Background Apps
Third-party overlays are notorious for interfering with the game's rendering pipeline. Try disabling the following before launching the game:
*   Discord Overlay
*   Steam/Epic Games Overlay
*   MSI Afterburner / RivaTuner
*   OBS (if using Game Capture mode)

### Adjust Virtual Memory (Page File)
Marvel Rivals is memory-intensive. If your system runs out of physical RAM and your Virtual Memory is too small or disabled, the game will crash to desktop (CTD) instantly. Ensure your Page File is set to "System Managed" on your fastest SSD.

### Check for Windows Updates
As of April 2026, certain Windows 11 security features like "Core Isolation" have been known to conflict with anti-cheat software used in competitive shooters. Ensure your OS is fully updated to the latest 2026 security patch to ensure compatibility with the [Marvel Rivals Official Client](https://www.marvelrivals.com/).

## FAQ

**Q: Why does Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt keep crashing during the MVP screen?**

A: This is often related to the high-quality assets used during the end-of-match animations. Lowering your "Global Illumination" or "Post-Processing" settings can usually resolve crashes that occur specifically during the victory/defeat transition.

**Q: Can a weak Power Supply Unit (PSU) cause Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt crashing?**

A: Yes. Marvel Rivals is a resource-heavy game. If your GPU draws more power than your PSU can provide during intense combat scenes, your computer may crash or restart to protect the hardware. Ensure your PSU wattage meets the requirements for your specific GPU.

**Q: Does the Blood Hunt update have a known memory leak?**

A: Some players in early 2026 reported a potential memory leak when playing for more than three hours consecutively. If you notice your performance degrading over time before a crash occurs, restarting the game client every few matches is a temporary workaround while waiting for a developer hotfix.

**Q: Is Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt crashing more common on Steam or Epic Games Store?**

A: Stability is generally similar across both platforms, though Steam users have more frequent access to "Beta" branches which may occasionally be less stable. Regardless of the launcher, ensuring your game files are verified after every update is the best way to prevent platform-specific crashes.
