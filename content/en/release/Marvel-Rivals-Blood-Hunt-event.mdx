## Overview of the Blood Hunt PvE Mode

The Blood Hunt event is a limited-time PvE experience that tasks players with pushing back waves of supernatural enemies across various Marvel-inspired locales, such as Grand Central Station. Unlike previous iterations, this mode emphasizes a "roguelite" progression style where players gather temporary buffs during a run while maintaining permanent gear and level progression outside of matches.

### Difficulty Levels and Structure

There are three primary difficulty settings available at launch. While "Normal" serves as a five-minute introduction to the mechanics, "Hard" and "Extreme" (and the eventual "Nightmare" tiers) require a much deeper understanding of character builds and gear ratings.

| Difficulty | Estimated Duration | Recommended Level | Key Features |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Normal** | 5-7 Minutes | Level 1-5 | Introduction to mechanics; Captain America boss encounter. |
| **Hard** | 10-15 Minutes | Level 10+ | Increased enemy health; more aggressive mini-bosses. |
| **Extreme** | 20+ Minutes | Level 20+ | Expanded story beats; multiple boss phases; high-tier gear drops. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the Normal difficulty to quickly farm shards and basic gear if you are starting with a new character. It is the most efficient way to reach level 5.

## Playable Heroes and Progression Changes

One of the most significant shifts in the **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt event** is how hero progression is handled. In previous modes, leveling up one character would often benefit your entire roster. In Blood Hunt, progression is strictly individual. If you reach level 10 with Moon Knight, your Blade or Thor will remain at level 1 until you put in the work with them.

### Featured Hero Roster

The event features a curated list of heroes, each with unique skill trees (Traits) designed specifically for PvE combat:

*   **Punisher:** Sustained DPS with a focus on firearms and explosives.
*   **Squirrel Girl:** High mobility and unique summon-based distractions.
*   **Jeff the Night Shark:** Support-focused utility with surprising survivability.

## Gear System: Smelting and Forging

The gear system adds a layer of RPG-style depth to the **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt event**. Enemies drop equipment that carries specific ratings and bonuses, such as increased damage against close-range foes or faster Ultimate ability regeneration.

### Managing Your Inventory

As you collect gear, your inventory will quickly fill with duplicates. This is where the Smelting and Forging systems become vital. You can "Smelt" unwanted items to receive Shards, which are the primary currency for crafting high-level equipment.

| Action | Result | Cost/Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Smelting** | Converts gear into Shards. | None (Destroys the item). |
| **Forging** | Creates a specific gear piece for a chosen hero. | 100 - 1,000+ Shards (Scales with Level). |
| **Shard Purchase** | Grants 100,000 Shards instantly. | 100 Units (In-game currency). |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when forging gear. The cost increases significantly as your character level rises. Forging a level 1 item is cheap (100 shards), but level 8+ items can cost 1,000 shards or more.

## Skill Trees and Traits

The "Traits" menu allows you to customize how your hero plays. Each hero has a branching skill tree that can fundamentally change their abilities. For example, Moon Knight players can choose between the "Crescent Dart" path (ranged focus) or the "Fist of the Moon God" path (melee focus).

Choosing the "Fist of the Moon God" trait replaces the standard Enk-dart throw with a powerful melee strike. It is important to focus on one side of the tree to maximize synergy, as upgrading both paths simultaneously often results in wasted points on abilities you have replaced. These points can be reset at any time, allowing for experimentation as you move from Normal to Extreme difficulties.

## Combat Mechanics: Healing and Arcana

To compensate for the increased difficulty in later stages, the developers have introduced a dedicated healing mechanic. By pressing Left on the D-Pad (or the corresponding keybind on PC), your hero will instantly consume a heal charge. You can hold up to three charges at once, and they regenerate over time or can be replenished through drops.

### Arcana Store Buffs

During a mission, you will encounter the Arcana Store. This allows you to select temporary "cards" that provide massive boosts for the duration of that specific run. Unlike the previous zombie mode, you cannot constantly refresh these choices, so your initial selection is crucial.

**Top Tier Arcana Buffs:**
1.  **Boss Damage:** Essential for clearing the final encounter quickly.
2.  **Ultimate Charge Speed:** Allows for more frequent use of high-impact abilities.
3.  **Total Output Boost:** A flat percentage increase to all damage and healing.

## Exclusive Rewards and Achievements

Participating in the **Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt event** grants access to a variety of cosmetic and functional rewards. Many of these are tied to specific achievements or hidden boss encounters.

*   **Kingpin Dethroned Accessory:** A prestigious item obtained by collecting shards from a hidden boss. Collecting enough shards allows you to craft a Gold version of this accessory.
*   **Bane of All Evil Title:** A gold-tier title awarded for completing the event's most difficult challenges.
*   **Character-Specific Titles:** Each featured hero has a unique title, such as "Midnight Mercenary" for Moon Knight or "Daywalking Duelist" for Blade.

## FAQ

**Q: How long does the Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt event last?**

A: The event is scheduled to run for 97 days from its launch in April 2026. However, the developers have indicated that this window may be extended based on player feedback and participation.

**Q: Do I lose my gear if I fail a mission?**

A: No. Any gear you have equipped or collected during the mission remains in your inventory even if your team is defeated. You only lose the temporary Arcana buffs gained during that specific run.

**Q: Can I share gear between different heroes?**

A: No. Gear is hero-specific. A weapon or accessory found while playing as Thor cannot be equipped by Punisher. However, you can smelt unwanted gear from any hero to get Shards, which can then be used to forge gear for your preferred character.

**Q: What is the best way to farm Shards quickly?**

A: The fastest way to gain Shards is to smelt duplicate gear found during Normal or Hard runs. If you have excess Units, you can also purchase 100,000 Shards for 100 Units in the event store, which is currently the most efficient way to "jump-start" a high-level character build.
