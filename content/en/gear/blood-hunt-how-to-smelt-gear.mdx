## Blood Hunt How to Smelt Gear: Exact Menu Steps

In Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt, smelting happens in the **Forge** area under the **Scrap** tab. You select unwanted items and convert them into **Uru shards**. Those shards are then used in the **Forge** tab to craft targeted gear pieces (like weapon, accessory, etc.) for a chosen hero and level.

Use this exact sequence every time you finish a run:

| Step | Action | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Open **Forge → Scrap** | This is the only place where smelting converts items to Uru shards. |
| **2** | Apply **Filter** (start with white + green) | Quickly isolates low-value gear that usually lacks strong extended effects. |
| **3** | Hit **Select All** | Mass-selects trash items in one click. |
| **4** | Manually deselect exceptions | Keep items for alt heroes or pieces with rare perk rolls. |
| **5** | Press **Smelt** | Converts selected gear into Uru shards immediately. |
| **6** | Go to **Forge** tab | Spend shards to craft missing slot upgrades. |
| **7** | Choose hero + slot + level | Targeting weak slots gives better gains than random equipping. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Don’t smelt purely by item level. A lower-level piece with the right perk (like close-range bonus for melee builds) can outperform a higher-level piece with poor synergy.

## What You Should Smelt vs What You Should Keep

Most players lose value because they smelt too aggressively or hoard everything. Use this decision matrix to stay efficient.

| Gear Type | Keep or Smelt | Rule of Thumb |
|---|---|---|
| **White rarity** | **Usually Smelt** | No extended effects; typically temporary filler only. |
| **Green rarity** | **Mostly Smelt** | Keep only if perk perfectly matches your build and slot is weak. |
| **Blue rarity** | **Conditional Keep** | Keep if it has strong effect tiers and real synergy. |
| **Purple rarity** | **Usually Keep** | Multiple extended effects; excellent candidates for core gear. |
| **Legendary/Orange** | **Strong Keep** | Evaluate perks and tiers before replacing. |
| **Alt-hero starter pieces** | **Keep selectively** | Save a few for heroes you plan to level next. |

### Read These 3 Stats Before Smelting Any Piece

1. **Perk relevance** – Does it match your build style (melee, ranged, skill-focused)?
2. **Extended effect count** – Higher rarity usually means more stacked effects.
3. **Effect tier quality** – A better tier on the same perk can dramatically increase value.

A common example: if both items boost close-range damage, the one with the higher effect tier may be a huge upgrade even at lower level.

## How to Spend Uru Shards After You Smelt Gear

Knowing **blood hunt how to smelt gear** is only half the process. The second half is spending shards intelligently. Don’t craft random slots. Craft the slot that is actively holding your build back.

### Priority Order for Crafting

| Priority | Slot Strategy | Why It’s Efficient |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Fix your weakest slot | Biggest immediate power increase for lowest complexity. |
| **2** | Target build-critical slot | Ex: melee build needs close-range damage rolls. |
| **3** | Craft at highest available level for main hero | Better baseline stat ceiling for current progression. |
| **4** | Craft low-level starter gear for alt heroes (optional) | Smooths the first few clears on secondary characters. |

### Practical Spending Rules

- Spend shards in **batches**, not one-by-one panic crafts.
- Stop crafting when you hit “good enough” synergy; don’t chase perfection too early.
- If a crafted piece is white or off-build, smelt it back and keep cycling.
- Re-check your equipped set after every 2–3 crafts so you don’t miss small upgrades.

## Build Synergy Matters More Than Raw Item Level

Players often equip the highest number and wonder why damage drops. In Blood Hunt, synergy can beat raw level, especially when perks multiply your chosen playstyle.

If your build is melee-focused:
- Prioritize close-range damage, sustain, and damage mitigation.
- Value higher-tier versions of those effects over random stat bumps.
- Avoid splitting too hard across unrelated bonuses.

If your build is ability-focused:
- Keep pieces that increase your core skill uptime or output.
- Don’t overcommit to ultimate-only bonuses unless your full setup supports it.

This is why **blood hunt how to smelt gear** should be tied directly to your build plan. Smelting is not just “inventory cleaning”; it’s your main resource engine for controlled progression.

## Best Farming Loop to Feed Your Smelt-and-Forge Cycle

To keep Uru income steady, run content where you can clear consistently and still get meaningful drops. A simple loop works well:

1. Clear your current stable difficulty quickly.
2. Collect all drops (end-of-wave auto-pickup helps).
3. Scrap low-value items in bulk.
4. Forge one targeted slot upgrade.
5. Test next difficulty breakpoint.
6. Repeat.

You also keep gear from failed runs, so testing harder tiers can still be profitable. Watch for loot goblin-style enemies (glowing runners): they can drop multiple gear pieces and are worth priority focus.

| Loop Phase | Time Goal | Output |
|---|---|---|
| **Run** | 10–25 min (depends on difficulty) | Gear drops, XP, progression familiarity |
| **Scrap pass** | 2–4 min | Uru shard conversion |
| **Forge pass** | 2–3 min | Targeted slot improvement |
| **Loadout review** | 1–2 min | Confirm synergy and replace weak pieces |

> **⚠️ Warning:** If your team is wiping repeatedly and boss enrage hits, stop spending revives freely. You may gain more value by ending the run, scrapping gains, and rebuilding.

## FAQ

**Q: In blood hunt how to smelt gear, where exactly is the smelt option?**  

A: Open **Forge**, switch to the **Scrap** tab, select unwanted gear, then press **Smelt**. The game converts selected items into Uru shards.

**Q: Should I smelt all white and green gear immediately?**  

A: Usually yes, but check for rare build-synergy exceptions. A few green pieces can be useful early if their perks match your exact setup.

**Q: Is forging worth it compared to waiting for drops?**  

A: Yes, especially for filling a weak slot. Forging gives targeted upgrades, while drops are less predictable. A balanced approach (farm + forge) is strongest.

**Q: What is the fastest way to improve after learning blood hunt how to smelt gear?**  

A: Build a repeatable loop: clear content you can finish reliably, bulk-smelt low-value drops, then craft one targeted slot at a time. This keeps power growth steady without wasting shards.
