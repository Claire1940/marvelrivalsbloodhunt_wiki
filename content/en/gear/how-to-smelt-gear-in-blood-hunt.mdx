## Understanding the Forge System in Blood Hunt

The Forge is the central hub for all gear management in the Blood Hunt event. Within this menu, you will find two primary functions: **Scrap** and **Forge**. While the names might seem self-explanatory, the synergy between these two tabs is what allows you to target specific "gaps" in your build.

When you learn how to smelt gear in blood hunt, you are essentially using the Scrap tab. Smelting takes your unwanted white, green, blue, and purple items and converts them into **Uru Shards**. These shards are the universal currency used in the Forge tab to create brand-new gear tailored to a specific character and slot.

### Gear Rarity and Perks

Before you start destroying items, it is important to understand what makes a piece of gear "good." Higher rarity items do not just offer higher base stats; they provide more "Extended Effects."

| Gear Rarity | Number of Extended Effects | Potential Perk Tiers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **White (Normal)** | 0 | N/A |
| **Green (Uncommon)** | 1 | D to C Tier |
| **Blue (Rare)** | 2 | C to B Tier |
| **Purple (Epic)** | 3 | B to A Tier |
| **Orange (Legendary)** | 4+ | A to S Tier |

> **💡 Tip:** Never equip an item based solely on its level or rating. A lower-level blue item with a "B Tier" bonus to close-range damage is often better than a higher-level purple item with irrelevant perks.

## How to Smelt Gear in Blood Hunt: Step-by-Step

Managing hundreds of items can be daunting, but the developers have included robust filtering tools to make the smelting process efficient. Follow these steps to clear your inventory and stock up on Uru shards.

### 1. Access the Forge Tab
Navigate to the main Blood Hunt event menu and select the **Forge** tab. By default, this usually opens the crafting screen, so you will need to click on the **Scrap** sub-tab at the top of the interface.

### 2. Use the Filter Tool
Instead of clicking every single item, use the **Filter** button. This allows you to highlight items based on rarity. For most players who have cleared Extreme mode, it is safe to filter for all "Normal" (White) and "Uncommon" (Green) gear.

### 3. Select All and Refine
Once the filters are applied, you can use the **Select All** button. However, exercise caution: gear is character-specific. If you plan on playing a different hero later, you might want to manually deselect high-rarity items for characters you haven't leveled yet.

### 4. Execute the Smelt
Once your items are selected, the screen will display the total amount of Uru shards you will receive. Click the **Smelt** button to finalize the process. The items will be destroyed instantly, and the shards will be added to your account.

## Forging: Turning Shards into Power

Once you know **how to smelt gear in blood hunt**, you can use your accumulated Uru shards to craft specific items. This is particularly useful if you have reached level 40 but are still wearing a level 20 accessory.

In the **Forge** tab, you can select:
1.  **The Character:** Choose which hero the gear is for.
2.  **The Slot:** Choose between weapons, armor, or accessories.
3.  **The Level:** The Forge defaults to the highest level your character has unlocked, but you can craft lower-level gear to help a friend or a secondary character.

| Crafting Level Target | Uru Shard Cost (Approx.) | Expected Rarity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1-10** | 500 - 1,500 | White / Green |
| **Level 11-30** | 3,000 - 6,000 | Green / Blue |
| **Level 31-50** | 8,000 - 12,000 | Blue / Purple |

> **Warning:** Forging is a "roll of the dice." You might spend 12,000 Uru shards and receive a White item with no perks. Be prepared to smelt your failed crafts to recoup a small portion of the cost.

## Maximizing Gear Drops during Runs

While smelting is great for recycling, the best gear comes from active gameplay. To get the most out of every run, you should aim for higher difficulties as soon as your power level allows.

*   **Normal Mode:** Drops Level 1-5 gear. Great for learning the Capwolf boss mechanics.
*   **Hard Mode:** Drops Level 8-12 gear. Introduces the Ratatoskr fight.
*   **Extreme Mode:** Drops Level 15-25 gear. This is the first major checkpoint where you will fight Dracula.
*   **Nightmare Mode:** Drops Level 30+ gear. This mode consists of five waves ending in a boss fight.

### Loot Goblins and Boss Mechanics
During your runs, keep an eye out for **Loot Goblins**—small, glowing yellow enemies that scurry across the arena. They do not attack, but they drop massive amounts of gear if killed before they disappear.

When fighting Dracula, remember that his mechanics are "gate-locked." When he disappears and summons floating swords, you must destroy all five swords to bring him back. If you fail to clear the boss within the hidden timer, he will enter an enraged state, doubling his damage output and potentially ending your run.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I smelt gear that is currently equipped?**

A: No, you must unequip an item before it will appear in the Scrap menu. Items currently in use will have a "Set" or "Equipped" icon on them in the inventory screen.

**Q: Does smelting a Purple item give more Uru shards than a White item?**

A: Yes. The amount of Uru shards returned is directly proportional to the item's rarity and level. Higher-tier gear provides a significantly larger yield when you perform a smelt.

**Q: How do I unlock the secret boss in Blood Hunt?**

A: To access the secret boss, you must complete the optional side objectives that appear on the top of your screen during an Extreme mode run before reaching Dracula's castle. This often involves destroying "Blood Root" flowers or other environmental targets.

**Q: Is there a limit to how much gear I can hold before I have to smelt?**

A: While there is a high capacity, you can easily pick up over 100 items in a few hours of grinding. It is best practice to learn **how to smelt gear in blood hunt** early so you don't hit the inventory cap mid-run, although the game will automatically collect any gear left on the ground at the end of a wave.
