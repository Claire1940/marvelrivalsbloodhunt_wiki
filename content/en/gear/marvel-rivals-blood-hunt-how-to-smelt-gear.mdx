## Understanding the Blood Hunt Gear System

Before diving into the mechanics of smelting, it is important to understand what you are actually handling. Gear in Blood Hunt is divided into four distinct categories that dictate your hero's combat effectiveness. Each piece of equipment drops with a specific level requirement and rarity, ranging from common to legendary.

| Gear Category | Primary Function | Optimization Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Weapon** | Increases base damage and ability power | Look for icons that match your hero's primary rotation. |
| **Armor** | Boosts health, resistance, and survivability | Prioritize damage reduction for frontline tanks. |
| **Accessory** | Provides utility and cooldown reduction | Focus on energy regeneration or movement speed. |
| **Exclusive** | Enhances specific hero-unique skills | These are build-defining items that alter ability behavior. |

As you progress through Normal, Hard, and Extreme difficulties, you will find your inventory filling up with low-level items. These items quickly become obsolete as your hero level increases, making them the perfect candidates for the smelting process.

## Marvel Rivals Blood Hunt: How to Smelt Gear Step-by-Step

Smelting is the process of destroying unwanted gear to receive **Uru Shards**, the primary currency used in the Forge. To begin this process, you must navigate to the Forge menu from the Blood Hunt main screen. It is generally recommended to keep your inventory clean to avoid missing out on powerful drops due to lack of space.

### The Smelting Process

1.  **Access the Forge Tab:** Open the Blood Hunt menu and select the "Forge" option at the top of the screen.
2.  **Select Smelt Menu:** Within the Forge, look for the smelting sub-menu which displays your current unequipped inventory.
3.  **Filter Your Items:** Use the filter tool to sort by rarity or level. This prevents you from accidentally selecting high-value items you might need for other heroes.
4.  **Lock Valuable Gear:** If you have a piece of gear with a perfect "Extended Effect" but you aren't high enough level to use it yet, use the **Lock** feature to remove it from the smelting pool.
5.  **Confirm Smelt:** Select the individual items or use the "Select All" function for lower rarities, then confirm to receive your Uru Shards.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Smelting is an irreversible process. Once an item is converted into Uru Shards, it cannot be recovered. Always double-check your "Exclusive" gear slots before mass-smelting.

## Forging New Gear with Uru Shards

Once you have accumulated enough Uru Shards from smelting, you can use the Forge to create brand-new equipment. This is the most effective way to hunt for specific gear pieces that haven't dropped naturally during matches. The Forge allows for a degree of "targeted RNG," letting you pick the hero and the gear slot you want to produce.

### How to Use the Forge Effectively

When forging, click on the large shard in the center of the menu. This opens a preset menu where you can select the hero and the specific part (Weapon, Armor, etc.) you are looking for. This ensures that every shard you spend contributes directly to your current goal.

| Forging Variable | Impact on Outcome | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Uru Shard Quantity** | Determines the number of items produced | Save shards for bulk forging to increase chances of high rarity. |
| **Gear Level** | Matches the gear to your current hero level | Only forge when you hit a level plateau to maximize stat gains. |
| **Quality RNG** | Determines if the item is Rare, Epic, or Legendary | Hover over the "Quality Details" icon to see current probability rates. |

## FAQ

**Q: Does smelting gear give different amounts of Uru Shards?**

A: Yes. The amount of Uru Shards you receive is determined by the item's level and rarity. A Legendary level 60 item will yield significantly more shards than a Common level 10 item.

**Q: Can I smelt gear that is currently equipped on another hero?**

A: No. The smelting menu automatically hides gear that is currently equipped on any of your heroes. If you want to smelt a piece of gear, you must first unequip it from the hero's loadout screen.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many Uru Shards I can hold?**

A: There is currently no known hard cap on Uru Shards, allowing you to save up large quantities for massive forging sessions when a new hero or gear tier is released.

**Q: How do I know which gear is safe to smelt?**

A: Generally, any gear that is 5-10 levels below your hero's current level is safe to smelt, unless it is a Legendary item with a unique effect that you haven't found a replacement for yet.
