5. **Smelt in batches**, then move immediately to Forge presets.  
6. **Craft only what your active hero build needs** right now.

## What You Should Smelt vs Keep

When players say smelting feels “RNG,” they’re usually missing a decision framework. You don’t need perfect rolls to make good calls—you need consistent criteria.

### Keep Priority Rules

- Gear that boosts your core damage skill or survival layer  
- Pieces with extended effects that match your current trait path  
- Higher-level items for heroes you actively play  
- Slot pieces where your current equipped item is clearly weak  

### Smelt Priority Rules

- Off-build effects for heroes you don’t intend to play soon  
- Redundant duplicates with weaker values  
- Low-rarity pieces with no meaningful effect interaction  
- Transitional items already replaced by superior versions  

| Gear signal | Keep or Smelt? | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Same slot, same effect, lower values | **Smelt** | Strict downgrade |
| Higher rarity but irrelevant effect | **Usually Smelt** | Power wasted if no synergy |
| Lower rarity but perfect build synergy | **Keep (short-term)** | Functional until better replacement |
| High-level item for planned secondary hero | **Keep + Lock** | Future account efficiency |
| Duplicate exclusive item with weaker extension | **Smelt** | Redundant inventory load |

> **💡 Tip:** In many cases, effect synergy beats small raw stat gains. A “weaker” piece can outperform a “stronger” one if it amplifies your main damage pattern.

If your aim is to optimize **marvel rivals smelt gear** decisions, think in “build outcomes,” not just color rarity.

## Spending Uru Shards in Forge Without Regret

Smelting is only half the equation. Many players correctly smelt trash, then waste all shards on unfocused crafting. Your Forge spending should follow progression stage.

### Stage-Based Forge Priorities

| Progress stage | Best shard focus | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Early Blood Hunt | Fill missing weak slots | Stabilize clear speed and survivability |
| Mid progression (Hard/Extreme) | Target core skill-enhancing gear | Build identity around 1-2 win conditions |
| Early Nightmare | Upgrade bottleneck slot first | Reduce deaths and DPS dips on bosses |
| High Nightmare push | Chase high-synergy extended effects | Fine-tune for consistency |

### Practical Forge Budgeting (Simple Rule)

Use a rough split:
- **60%** shards for your main hero’s weakest slot  
- **25%** for your second-worst slot  
- **15%** reserve for later rolls or emergency upgrades  

This avoids the common trap of overinvesting one category while leaving a critical defensive slot underpowered.

Another important note: Forge allows selecting target parameters (hero/part preset). Use that every time. Random unfocused forging is the fastest way to flatten your shard growth curve.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best way to start with marvel rivals smelt gear as a beginner?**

A: Start with a safety-first routine: evaluate by slot, lock likely upgrades, then smelt low-rarity off-build pieces. Spend shards only on your weakest active slot for your main hero until your clears feel stable.

**Q: Should I smelt all duplicates immediately?**

A: Not immediately. Compare base values and extended effects first. If a duplicate has weaker values and no unique synergy, it’s a good smelt target. If it supports a secondary build or hero, lock it.

**Q: Is rarity more important than effect synergy?**

A: Rarity helps, but synergy often decides real performance. A lower-rarity piece aligned with your Traits/Arcana can outperform a higher-rarity item with the wrong effect profile.

**Q: How often should I forge after smelting?**

A: Forge in controlled batches after each farming cycle, not every single run. Batch upgrades make it easier to track stat changes and avoid impulsive shard spending.
