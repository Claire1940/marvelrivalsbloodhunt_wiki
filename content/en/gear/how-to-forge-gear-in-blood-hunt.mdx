## Understanding the Blood Hunt Gear System

Before you dive into the Forge, you must understand what makes a piece of gear "good." In Blood Hunt, gear is character-specific. While you might find drops for other heroes occasionally, your primary source of equipment will be the character you are currently playing. Gear is divided into several rarities, and unlike many other RPGs, the raw "Rating" or level of the item isn't the only factor you should consider.

The most important aspect of any gear piece is its **Extended Effects**. These are additional perks that provide massive percentage-based buffs to your abilities, such as life leech, cooldown reduction, or bonus damage against close-range enemies.

### Gear Rarity and Effect Slots

| Gear Rarity | Number of Extended Effects | Potential Stat Tiers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **White (Common)** | 0 | None |
| **Green (Uncommon)** | 1 | D to C Tier |
| **Blue (Rare)** | 2 | C to B Tier |
| **Purple (Epic)** | 3 | B to A Tier |
| **Orange (Legendary)** | 3+ | A to S Tier |

As shown in the table above, higher rarity gear doesn't just offer better base stats; it offers more slots for powerful perks. A level 32 Blue item with a "B Tier" damage perk is often significantly stronger than a level 40 White item with no perks at all.

## How to Forge Gear in Blood Hunt: Step-by-Step

To access the crafting system, navigate to the **Forge** tab from the main event menu. Inside, you will find two primary sub-tabs: **Scrap** and **Forge**. You cannot create new items without first destroying the ones you don't need.

### Step 1: Collecting Uru Shards (Scrapping)
Every run you complete will likely leave your inventory cluttered with dozens of White and Green items. To get the materials needed to forge, you must "Smelt" these items into **Uru Shards**.

1.  Open the **Forge** menu and select the **Scrap** tab.
2.  Use the **Filter** button to quickly select all "Normal" and "Green" rarity items.
3.  Review the list to ensure you aren't accidentally scrapping high-tier gear for characters you plan to play later.
4.  Hit **Smelt** to convert the selected gear into Uru Shards.

### Step 2: Crafting New Equipment
Once you have a healthy stockpile of Uru Shards, move over to the **Forge** tab. This is where you actually execute the process of **how to forge gear in blood hunt**.

1.  **Select the Hero:** Choose the character you want to craft gear for.
2.  **Select the Slot:** You can choose to forge specific pieces, such as Weapons, Armor, or Accessories. This is vital for "plugging gaps" in your build if a specific slot is lagging behind in level.
3.  **Set the Level:** The Forge defaults to the highest level possible based on your character's current progression. However, you can lower the level if you are crafting starter gear for a secondary character to save on costs.
4.  **Confirm and Roll:** Forging is a "roll of the dice." You spend a set amount of Uru Shards (e.g., 6,000 for a high-level piece) to generate a random rarity item.

> **💡 Tip:** Don't be discouraged by White or Green results from the Forge. Crafting is a volume game; you forge multiple items, keep the Purple or Legendary ones with the right perks, and scrap the rest to recoup some of your Uru Shards.

## Optimizing Your Forged Gear

Learning **how to forge gear in blood hunt** is only the first half of the battle. The second half is knowing which pieces to keep. When you forge a new item, immediately inspect its **Extended Effects**. 

Stat values are categorized by tiers (D, C, B, A, and S). A "B Tier" perk might offer 250% bonus damage, while a "D Tier" version of the same perk only offers 120%. Even if you forge two Blue items of the same level, one might be vastly superior because of the Tier of its internal rolls.

### Priority Stats for Popular Builds

| Build Type | Priority Forging Stats | Recommended Hero |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Melee DPS** | Life Leech, Close-Range Damage, Attack Speed | Moon Knight |
| **Ranged Sniper** | Crit Damage, Headshot Bonus, Cooldown Reduction | Punisher |
| **Skill Spammer** | Ultimate Charge Rate, Skill Power, Energy Regen | Squirrel Girl |

Always compare your forged results to your currently equipped gear. The game UI will helpfully display a "Set" tag on items you are currently wearing, allowing for a side-by-side comparison of percentages.

## Resource Management and Efficiency

Uru Shards are a finite resource, especially in the early stages of the Blood Hunt event. To maximize your efficiency while learning **how to forge gear in blood hunt**, follow these management rules:

*   **Don't Forge Too Early:** Your forged gear level is capped by your character's progression. It is better to clear Normal and Hard modes using found drops, then start forging heavily once you reach Extreme or Nightmare difficulties where the level ceiling is higher.
*   **Target Accessories First:** Accessories often provide the most unique utility buffs. If your damage feels fine but you're dying too quickly, forge Accessories to look for Life Leech or Damage Mitigation perks.
*   **The Loot Goblin Factor:** Keep an eye out for "Loot Goblins" (yellow glowing enemies) during your runs. These enemies drop a massive amount of gear when killed, providing you with more "scrap fodder" for your Uru Shard bank.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when using the "Select All" feature in the Scrap menu. If you have obtained Legendary (Orange) gear for a character you aren't currently playing, the filter might still include it if you haven't locked the item. Always double-check before smelting!

The quality of the gear you can forge is directly tied to the difficulty levels you have cleared. You cannot forge level 40 gear if you have only cleared Normal mode. 

| Difficulty | Typical Gear Drop Level | Forging Unlock Potential |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Normal** | 1 - 5 | Low (Basic White/Green) |
| **Hard** | 8 - 15 | Medium (Green/Blue) |
| **Extreme** | 16 - 30 | High (Blue/Purple) |
| **Nightmare** | 40+ | Elite (Purple/Legendary) |

To efficiently progress, find a "baseline" difficulty you can clear comfortably. Farm that difficulty until your gear slots are filled with Blue or Purple items, then use the Forge to replace any remaining low-level pieces before attempting the next difficulty tier.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I share forged gear between different characters?**

A: No. Gear in Blood Hunt is character-specific. If you forge a pair of knuckles for Moon Knight, they cannot be equipped by the Punisher. However, you can use Uru Shards earned on one character to forge gear for another character by switching the hero selection in the Forge menu.

**Q: What is the best way to get Uru Shards for forging?**

A: The fastest way to get Uru Shards is to run Extreme difficulty missions and focus on killing every enemy. At the end of the wave, all dropped gear is automatically added to your inventory. You can then go to the Forge and scrap all the low-tier items you picked up.

**Q: Does the rarity of the item I forge depend on my level?**

A: No, the rarity (White, Blue, Purple, etc.) is random every time you forge. However, the *level* of the item is determined by your character's current level and progression. To get the most out of **how to forge gear in blood hunt**, wait until you are a higher level so you aren't wasting shards on low-level equipment.

**Q: Should I use my Uru Shards to forge gear for characters I haven't played yet?**

A: Yes! This is a great strategy. If you have a surplus of shards from your main character, you can forge a full set of level 1 or level 5 gear for a new hero. This gives them a massive head start, allowing them to skip the tedious early-game grind.
