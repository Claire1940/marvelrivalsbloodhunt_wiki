## What Smelting Does in Blood Hunt (and Why It Matters)

In Blood Hunt mode, smelting breaks unwanted gear into crafting currency (commonly tracked as Uru-related material/shards). That currency is what fuels focused crafting at your best available level.

A lot of players over-prioritize world drops. Drops are important, but crafted gear tends to be more reliable for endgame progression because you can target:

- The **character**
- The **slot/type** (like armor or a specific item category)
- The **highest craftable level** (“Best” level option)

Here’s the practical difference:

| Source | Main Strength | Main Weakness | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Enemy/world drops** | Free and frequent at higher Nightmare | Often below your personal level | Early upgrades, lucky spikes |
| **Crafted gear** | Can be crafted at your top eligible level | Costs smelted resources | Build-fixing and endgame min-max |
| **Legendary currency drops** | Enables accessory crafting | Not wearable gear immediately | Long-term accessory progress |

> **⚠️ Warning:** A “legendary” color pickup is not always a legendary gear piece. Some drops are legendary crafting currency for accessories.

If your build is stuck with one underleveled slot (common with chest/armor pieces), smelting and crafting is usually how you solve it without relying on luck.

## marvel rivals how to smelt gear: Step-by-Step Forge Workflow

This is the repeatable routine experienced players use when they want efficient progression instead of random inventory clutter.

### 1) Filter and bulk-smelt low rarity gear
Open Forge, filter for low-rarity pieces (normal/green/blue), then mass-select and smelt.

### 2) Manually review remaining gear
After bulk cleanup, inspect higher rarity items and smelt pieces that do not fit your current build plan.

### 3) Pick the exact craft target
Choose your current character and the slot you need most (for example, armor/chest if survivability is weak).

### 4) Set craft level to highest available
Scroll to your best craftable level option before confirming.

### 5) Craft in batches (usually 10 at a time)
Batch crafting gives enough rolls to compare outcomes and reduces resource waste from one-off gambling.

| Step | Action | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **Filter junk** | Smelt normal/green/blue first | Fastest way to build currency |
| **Review purples/legendaries** | Keep only build-relevant perks | Prevents inventory bloat |

| **Craft at Best level** | Prioritize highest item level | Better long-term scaling |
| **Batch craft** | Roll multiple items per cycle | Better odds to find usable perks |

> **💡 Tip:** Keep one “baseline” piece per slot before heavy smelting, so you don’t accidentally delete your best fallback item.

## Build Synergy Rules: Don’t Equip the Wrong Legendary

A legendary can look amazing and still hurt your performance if its perks support a different trait branch. In practice:

- If a perk line is grayed out for your current allocation, you gain less value
- An A-tier or S-tier perk with no active synergy can lose to a lower-tier perk that supports your active build path
- Item level, perk tier, and perk relevance all matter together

Use this quick scoring model before equipping:

| Check | Question | Keep or Smelt Signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Item Level** | Is it at/near your best available level? | Keep if equal/higher and useful |
| **Perk Activation** | Are key perks active for your current trait tree? | Smelt if multiple perks are inactive |
| **Role Fit** | Does it solve damage, sustain, or cooldown gaps? | Keep if it fills a real weakness |
| **Comparison Value** | Is it better than your current slot piece? | Smelt duplicates with no upgrade edge |

> **💡 Tip:** Evaluate upgrades by “effective output” (DPS uptime, healing uptime, durability uptime), not just rarity color.
