## How to Smelt Gear in Marvel Rivals

The smelting process is handled entirely within the **Forge** menu. This is where you convert your excess inventory into **Uru Shards**, the primary currency used for crafting new, high-tier equipment. Follow these steps to efficiently manage your inventory:

1.  **Navigate to the Forge Tab:** From the main Blood Hunt menu, select the "Forge" option.
2.  **Access the Smelting Menu:** Look for the smelting sub-section where your unequipped gear is displayed.
3.  **Filter Your Inventory:** Use the filter tool to narrow down items by rarity or level. This prevents you from accidentally selecting items you might want to save for other builds.
4.  **Lock Valuable Items:** Before you begin a mass smelt, ensure your best pieces are "Locked." Locked items will not appear in the smelting selection screen.
5.  **Select and Smelt:** You can select items individually or use the "Select All" button for a quick clear. Once selected, confirm the action to receive your Uru Shards.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Smelting is an irreversible process. Always double-check your locked items and filters before confirming a bulk smelt to avoid losing a rare exclusive item.

## Advanced Optimization: Extended Effects and Stats

The reason you want to master the loop of smelting and forging is to hunt for **Extended Effects**. While base effects (like flat damage or health) are standard, higher rarity gear unlocks additional slots for unique enhancements. These can range from increased damage against "Healthy" enemies (those with high HP) to cooldown resets on kills.

### Understanding Key Keywords
When reviewing your gear stats, you may encounter terms that aren't immediately clear. The game provides tooltips, but here is a quick reference for common 2026 gear descriptors:
*   **Close-range Enemy:** Typically refers to enemies within a 5-8 meter radius of your hero.
*   **Healthy Enemy:** Generally refers to enemies with more than 80% or 90% of their maximum health remaining.
*   **Weakened Enemy:** Enemies currently affected by status ailments or crowd control.

By aligning these gear effects with your **Traits** (the hero-specific skill tree) and **Arcana** (match-specific upgrades), you can create synergistic loops. For example, a Punisher build focusing on his sentry turret upgrade benefits immensely from gear that reduces cooldowns when his turret scores a finishing blow.

## Difficulty Scaling and Loot Quality

The quality of gear you receive—and subsequently the efficiency of **how to smelt gear marvel rivals** offers—depends on the difficulty level you play. Blood Hunt features a tiered progression system:

1.  **Normal:** The entry point where you learn the basics and fight Capwolf.
2.  **Hard & Extreme:** Introduces more phases, blood crystal objectives, and the Dracula boss fight.
3.  **Nightmare 1-100+:** The ultimate endgame. These levels consist of five intense phases. As you clear a Nightmare level, you unlock several tiers above it (e.g., beating Nightmare 5 unlocks up to Nightmare 10).

Higher Nightmare levels do not just offer more health to enemies; they provide the highest level gear drops in the game. If you find yourself stuck on a specific Nightmare tier, it is often a sign that you need to return to the Forge, smelt your outdated gear, and aim for higher-rarity items with better stat rolls.

> **💡 Tip:** If you are playing solo, remember to uncheck the "Auto-fill Teammates" option. Enemies and bosses will have reduced health to compensate for the lack of a full squad, making it easier to farm gear if you have a highly optimized build.

## Integrating Arcana and Traits

Smelting and forging are only two parts of the trinity of power in Marvel Rivals. To truly dominate, you must balance your gear with:

*   **Traits:** You earn assignable points until level 60. These can fundamentally change how abilities work, such as turning a manual ability into an automated one.
*   **Arcane Realm:** This is a global level that carries over across all heroes. Each level grants a point to put into general stat boosts that apply to every match you play.
*   **In-Match Arcana:** These are the random card choices you get after clearing waves. Your gear should complement these choices; if you tend to pick Arcana that boosts movement speed, look for gear that adds damage based on your current velocity.
